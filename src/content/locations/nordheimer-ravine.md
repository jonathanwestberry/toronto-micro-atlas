The entrance to Nordheimer Ravine sits just below the St. Clair streetcar tracks, almost invisible until you are already past the threshold. One step off the sidewalk and the sound envelope changes: traffic fades, replaced by creek noise and the particular acoustics of a narrow valley.

Castle Frank Creek runs through here, a tributary of the Don that survived underpinning. The valley is steep-sided and narrow compared to the Rosedale ravine system further east; it feels more enclosed, more accidental. The trail drops fast.

*Observation conditions: morning, dry. Entry grade: steep. Canopy: partial (edge zone).*
