The Old Mill Bridge crosses the Humber at one of its widest points within the city. The river has room here: a floodplain that resisted development, valley walls that recede enough to let the sky in. Standing at mid-span, the Kingsway neighbourhood is visible to the north but distant; to the south, the river bends toward the lake and the banks go low and marshy.

The old mill ruins are upstream, barely visible. What remains here is just the crossing, and the river doing its slow work below.

*Observation conditions: afternoon, wind from the southwest. Water level: moderate. Bridge deck: open.*
