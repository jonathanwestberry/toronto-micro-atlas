Standing at the centre of the Glen Road Pedestrian Bridge, the Rosedale street grid disappears. The ravine opens below and to the east, dense with silver maple and black walnut. The Don River tributary that carved this valley is buried further downstream, but here the valley is still open, still itself.

The bridge connects two cliff-edge streets, but what it actually does is suspend you between two landscape registers: the flat, paved city above and the older, deeper topography below. Neither shore is exactly urban. Both ends of the bridge feel like thresholds.

*Observation conditions: overcast, late afternoon. Canopy: closed. Water audible from below.*
