Crothers Woods sits on the east bank of the Don River where the valley is wide enough to hold a former brickyard. The entrance off Millwood Road is not dramatic (a gap in a fence line, a descending gravel path), but within a hundred metres the valley has absorbed you and the industrial past of the site begins to assert itself through the vegetation.

Cudmore Creek enters the Don nearby. The floodplain is broad and cottonwood-heavy. The trail system here connects north to Leaside and south toward the Brickworks; Crothers is the middle passage, less visited than either end.

*Observation conditions: morning, spring. Ground: soft. Canopy: opening.*
